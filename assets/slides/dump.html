Timeline

January 2002

Republia launches tender for Niibata International Airport

June 2002

AeroSonic and RIAC execute 30-year concession agreement for Niibata International Airport

February 2013

RIAC writes to AeroSonic congratulating it for its performance.

April 2013

RIAC expresses its concern about increased night-time air traffic at Niibata.

April 2013

In repsonse to RIAC's concern, AeroSonic adds soundproofing barriers at Niibata.

May 2014

Protest staged at Niibata International Airport

October 2014

International Airports Curfew Act (IACA) passed into law, prohibiting landings after 10:00pm.

May 2015

Internal emails at RIAC record RIAC's view that awarding AeroSonic the Kawaya concession would result in a 'major public outcry', and confirm that the Minister intends to discuss Kawaya with Yotsuba, a company for which his brother-in-law works.

June 2015

MIFFI fines AeroSonic for breaches of IACA.

October 2015

MIFFI cancels concession agreement.

July 2015

MIFFI and RIAC open tender for Kawaya airport.

May 2016

Kawaya airport concession awarded to Yotsuba Transport Co.
